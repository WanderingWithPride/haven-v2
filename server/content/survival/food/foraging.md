# Food Foraging & Preservation

## The Universal Edibility Test
**NEVER eat anything unknown without testing. Many plants are deadly.**

1. **Test on skin:** Crush, rub on inner wrist. Wait 15 min. Reaction = avoid.
2. **Test on lip:** Touch to outer lip. Wait 15 min.
3. **Test on tongue:** Place small piece on tongue. Wait 15 min.
4. **Chew and spit:** Chew a tiny amount, spit out. Wait 15 min.
5. **Swallow tiny amount:** Wait 8 hours. Any nausea, cramps = avoid.
6. **If no reaction:** Eat small portions, gradually increasing.

## Safe Common Wild Foods

### Plants
- **Dandelions:** All parts edible. Leaves raw or cooked, roots as tea.
- **Clover:** Flowers and leaves edible.
- **Cattails:** "Supermarket of the wild." Shoots, roots, pollen all edible.
- **Pine needles:** Tea rich in Vitamin C. Avoid yew trees (poisonous).
- **Bamboo shoots:** Boil to remove toxins. Edible after cooking.
- **Coconut:** Water, meat, oil all nutritious.

### Protein Sources
- **Insects:** Grasshoppers, crickets, ants (remove heads), grubs: roast first
- **Worms:** Squeeze out gut contents, roast or boil
- **Fish:** From clean water, cook thoroughly
- **Snails:** Purge for 2 days, cook thoroughly (parasites)
- **Bird eggs:** Always edible when fresh

### Avoid These
- ❌ Mushrooms (unless 100% identified: too many deadly lookalikes)
- ❌ Plants with milky sap (exceptions exist but too risky)
- ❌ Red berries (many are toxic; some are safe but don't guess)
- ❌ Plants with umbrella-like flower clusters (hemlock family)
- ❌ Beans/seeds in pods (many contain toxins unless cooked properly)

## Food Preservation Methods

### Drying/Dehydrating
- Cut food into thin strips (¼ inch)
- Hang in direct sunlight with airflow
- Can use fire smoke (adds flavor + antimicrobial)
- Done when food snaps, doesn't bend
- Store in airtight container
- Shelf life: weeks to months

### Smoking
1. Build a smoking rack above a fire pit (3-4 feet high)
2. Use hardwood for smoke (oak, hickory, mesquite)
3. **Do NOT use resinous wood** (pine, spruce: toxic compounds)
4. Keep fire smoldering (not flaming)
5. Smoke for 12-24 hours
6. Shelf life: 1-2 weeks without refrigeration

### Salt Curing
1. Pack meat/fish completely in salt
2. Ensure ALL surfaces are covered
3. Store in cool, dry place
4. Rinse and soak in water before eating
5. Shelf life: months

### Fermentation
- **Sauerkraut:** Shred cabbage + 2% salt by weight → press into jar → wait 1-4 weeks
- **Pickles:** Vegetables + saltwater brine (3-5% salt)
- Fermented foods provide probiotics and preserve nutrition

## Cooking Without Utensils
- **Hot rock cooking:** Heat rocks in fire, drop into water-filled container (NOT river rocks: they can explode)
- **Clay-bake:** Wrap in thick layer of clay, place in coals
- **Green stick roasting:** Skewer on fresh (green) wood stick over flame
- **Pit cooking:** Dig hole, line with hot rocks, add food wrapped in leaves, cover

## Calorie Priorities
Average survival need: ~2000 calories/day (more in cold/active situations)
- **Fat has 9 calories per gram** (most efficient survival food)
- Protein and carbs: 4 calories per gram
- Prioritize calorie-dense foods: nuts, seeds, animal fat, roots
