# Shelter Building

## Priorities in Survival (Rule of 3s)
- 3 minutes without air
- 3 hours without shelter (extreme weather)
- 3 days without water
- 3 weeks without food

**Shelter is often your #1 priority** after immediate medical needs.

## Site Selection
Choose a location that is:
- ✅ Protected from wind
- ✅ Away from water (flooding risk) but near water source
- ✅ On high ground (cold air sinks)
- ✅ Not under dead trees/branches
- ✅ Near building materials
- ✅ Visible to rescuers (if you want to be found)
- ❌ NOT in a dry riverbed
- ❌ NOT under cliff overhangs (rockfall)

## Type 1: Debris Hut (Best Emergency Shelter)
1. Find a ridgepole: strong, 3x your body length
2. Prop one end on a stump/rock at 3-foot height
3. Lay sticks against ridgepole at 45° angle on both sides
4. Layer debris (leaves, grass, ferns) 3-4 feet thick
5. Fill inside floor with dry debris for insulation
6. Make it JUST big enough: body heat retention is key

**Body heat alone can keep interior 30°F warmer than outside.**

## Type 2: Tarp/Poncho Shelter
### A-Frame
1. Tie cordage between two trees
2. Drape tarp over cord
3. Stake corners to ground
4. Angle for water runoff

### Lean-To
1. Tie tarp high on one side
2. Stake low on other side
3. Open side faces away from wind

## Type 3: Snow Shelter (Cold Climate)
### Quinzee
1. Pile snow in mound 6+ feet high
2. Wait 2 hours for snow to sinter (settle/bond)
3. Hollow out: walls should be 12-18 inches thick
4. Punch ventilation hole with stick
5. Door should be lower than sleeping platform (cold air sinks)

**Interior temperature stays near 32°F (0°C) even if outside is -40°.**

## Type 4: Urban Shelter (Disaster)
When buildings are damaged:
- Check structural integrity before entering
- Look for interior rooms (most protected)
- Avoid rooms with large windows
- Block drafts with plastic, cardboard, blankets
- Create a "warm room": smallest room, seal doors/windows
- Insulate floor (sleeping on concrete = hypothermia)

## Insulation Priorities
The ground steals MORE heat than the air:
1. **Ground insulation first**: pile leaves, pine needles, cardboard (4+ inches)
2. **Overhead protection**: rain/wind defense
3. **Side walls**: wind block

## Essential Shelter Kit
If you can prepare:
- Space/emergency blanket (weighs nothing, reflects 90% body heat)
- 50 feet paracord
- Small tarp or heavy-duty garbage bag
- Duct tape
