# Fire Making Methods

## Why Fire Matters
Fire provides: **warmth, water purification, cooking, signaling, light, morale, and protection from animals**.

## Method 1: Matches & Lighter (Always Carry)
- Store matches in waterproof container
- Waterproof matches by dipping in wax
- Keep lighters dry: they work even after getting wet (let dry first)

## Method 2: Ferro Rod (Best Survival Tool)
1. Hold rod steady against tinder
2. Scrape striker DOWN the rod firmly
3. Sparks at 3000°C: hot enough to ignite most tinder
4. Works when wet, lasts thousands of strikes

## Method 3: Bow Drill (Primitive)
Materials needed:
- **Fireboard:** flat, dry softwood (cedar, willow, poplar)
- **Spindle:** straight, dry stick, pencil-width, 1-2 feet long
- **Bow:** curved stick with cordage
- **Socket:** hardwood or stone with depression for top of spindle
- **Ember tray:** thin piece under notch

Steps:
1. Cut V-notch in fireboard leading to small depression
2. Place ember tray under notch
3. Wrap bowstring around spindle
4. Press spindle into fireboard depression with socket
5. Saw bow back and forth: steady, increasing speed
6. Black dust collects in notch → ember forms
7. Transfer ember to tinder bundle, blow gently into flame

## Method 4: Battery + Steel Wool
1. Stretch fine steel wool (0000 grade)
2. Touch both battery terminals to steel wool
3. Wool ignites instantly
4. Transfer to tinder

## Tinder (Best → Worst)
1. Dryer lint (carry some always!)
2. Cotton balls + petroleum jelly
3. Birch bark (contains flammable oils)
4. Dry grass bundles
5. Fine wood shavings
6. Dried pine needles
7. Paper/cardboard
8. Char cloth (pre-charred cotton)

## Building the Fire

| Stage | Material | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **1** | TINDER | smallest, most flammable material |
| **2** | KINDLING | pencil-thin sticks, small twigs |
| **3** | FUEL WOOD | wrist-thick sticks |
| **4** | LOGS | arm-thick wood for sustained burn |

### Fire Lay Structures
- **Teepee:** lean kindling against each other over tinder. Good for starting.
- **Log Cabin:** stack wood in squares. Burns evenly, good for cooking.
- **Long Fire:** two parallel logs with fire between. Even heat for sleeping.
- **Star Fire:** push 5+ long logs toward center. Low fuel use, burns all night.

## Fire Safety
- Clear area 10 feet around fire
- Never leave unattended
- Keep water/dirt nearby to extinguish
- Build fire pit: ring of rocks, dig slight depression
- Fully extinguish: drown, stir, drown, feel for heat

## Signal Fires
Three fires in a triangle = universal distress signal
- Add green leaves/grass for smoke
- Prepare materials in advance for quick ignition
