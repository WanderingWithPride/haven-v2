# Navigation Without GPS

## Using the Sun

### Finding Direction
- **Sun rises in the EAST, sets in the WEST** (both hemispheres)
- At noon (solar noon), the sun is due SOUTH (Northern Hemisphere) or due NORTH (Southern Hemisphere)

### Shadow Stick Method
1. Place a stick vertically in the ground
2. Mark the tip of the shadow with a stone
3. Wait 15-30 minutes
4. Mark the new shadow tip
5. Draw a line between the two marks
6. **This line runs EAST-WEST** (first mark = West, second = East)
7. Stand with West on your left = you face North

### Watch Compass (Analog Watch)
**Northern Hemisphere:**
1. Point the hour hand at the sun
2. Bisect the angle between hour hand and 12 o'clock
3. This bisection points SOUTH

**Southern Hemisphere:**
1. Point 12 o'clock at the sun
2. Bisect the angle between 12 and hour hand
3. This bisection points NORTH

## Using Stars

### Northern Hemisphere: Polaris (North Star)
1. Find the Big Dipper (Ursa Major)
2. The two stars at the end of the "cup" point to Polaris
3. Polaris is the last star in the Little Dipper's handle
4. **Polaris = True North** (within 1°)
5. It's the only star that doesn't move throughout the night

### Southern Hemisphere: Southern Cross
1. Find the Southern Cross (Crux) — 4 bright stars in cross shape
2. Extend the long axis of the cross 4.5x its length downward
3. Drop a perpendicular line to the horizon
4. **That point on the horizon = South**

## Using Natural Signs
These are APPROXIMATIONS, not reliable alone:
- Moss tends to grow on NORTH side of trees (Northern Hemisphere) — but is unreliable in dense forest
- Ant hills often have their entrance on the SOUTH side (warmer)
- Snow melts first on SOUTH-facing slopes
- Spider webs often face SOUTH
- Tree rings may be wider on the SOUTH side

## Distance Estimation

### Pace Count
1. Walk a known 100-meter distance normally
2. Count your paces (each time your LEFT foot hits ground)
3. Your count ÷ 100m = your "pace factor"
4. Average person: ~65 paces per 100m
5. Keep count using beads, knots, or pebbles

### Estimating Distance by Sound
| Sound | Approximate Distance |
|-------|---------------------|
| Shout | 300m |
| Car horn | 1 km |
| Gunshot | 2-3 km |
| Thunder | count seconds × 340m |

## Map Reading Basics (If You Have a Map)
- **Contour lines close together** = steep terrain
- **Contour lines far apart** = flat terrain
- **Blue** = water features
- **Green** = vegetation
- **Brown** = contour lines (elevation)
- Orient map by aligning features you can see

## Coordinate Systems
- **Latitude:** 0° at equator, 90° at poles. North/South.
- **Longitude:** 0° at Greenwich. East/West.
- **Decimal format:** 28.6139, 77.2090 (Delhi)
- **DMS format:** 28°36'50"N, 77°12'32"E
- Conversion: decimal = degrees + minutes/60 + seconds/3600

## Emergency: Leaving Trail
If moving through unknown terrain:
- Mark your path (stacked stones, broken branches, scratches on trees)
- Note landmarks (distinctive rocks, rivers, ridges)
- Travel along ridges when possible (better view, easier navigation)
- Follow water downstream — it usually leads to civilization
