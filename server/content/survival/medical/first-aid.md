# First Aid Essentials

## STOP THE BLEED
The #1 killer in emergencies is uncontrolled bleeding.

### Direct Pressure
1. Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth or bandage
2. Do NOT remove the first cloth — add more on top
3. Maintain pressure for at least 10 minutes
4. Elevate the wound above heart level if possible

### Tourniquet (Life-threatening limb bleeding)
1. Place 2-3 inches above the wound (NOT on a joint)
2. Tighten until bleeding stops
3. Note the TIME of application
4. Do NOT remove — let medical professionals handle it
5. Improvised: use belt, torn cloth, stick to twist tight

## CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)
1. Check responsiveness — tap shoulders, shout
2. Call for help
3. **30 chest compressions** — push hard and fast, center of chest, 2 inches deep
4. **2 rescue breaths** — tilt head back, lift chin, seal mouth
5. Repeat 30:2 cycle
6. Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute (think "Stayin' Alive" beat)

## Wound Care
- **Clean** with clean water (no soap in deep wounds)
- **Remove debris** gently
- **Apply antibiotic** ointment if available
- **Cover** with sterile bandage
- **Change** dressing daily
- Signs of infection: redness spreading, warmth, swelling, pus, fever

## Fractures & Splinting
1. **Do NOT try to straighten** the bone
2. Pad the splint material (sticks, boards)
3. Immobilize the joints above AND below the fracture
4. Tie firmly but check circulation (feeling, warmth, color in fingers/toes)
5. Cold pack for swelling (20 min on, 20 min off)

## Burns
- **Cool** immediately with cool (not cold) running water for 10-20 minutes
- **Do NOT** use ice, butter, or toothpaste
- **Cover** loosely with clean, non-stick bandage
- **Do NOT** pop blisters
- Severe burns: cover with clean dry cloth, treat for shock

## Shock
Signs: pale skin, rapid breathing, confusion, weakness
1. Lay person down, elevate legs 12 inches
2. Keep warm with blankets
3. Do NOT give food or water
4. Monitor breathing continuously

## Choking
**Conscious adult:**
1. 5 back blows between shoulder blades
2. 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)
3. Alternate until object is expelled

**Unconscious:** Begin CPR, check mouth for visible object before breaths

## Common Medications Reference
| Medicine | Use | Adult Dose |
|----------|-----|-----------|
| Paracetamol/Acetaminophen | Pain, fever | 500-1000mg every 6hr |
| Ibuprofen | Pain, swelling, fever | 200-400mg every 8hr |
| Loperamide | Diarrhea | 4mg initially, then 2mg |
| ORS (Oral Rehydration) | Dehydration | 1L water + 6 tsp sugar + ½ tsp salt |
| Antihistamine | Allergic reaction | Per package (diphenhydramine 25-50mg) |
| Activated Charcoal | Poisoning (not all) | 1g per kg body weight |
